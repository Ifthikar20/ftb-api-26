# Objection Handling

## Price Objections
- **"It's too expensive."**
  → "I totally hear you. Quick question — if the ROI worked out and you were saving more than you spent, would price still be the blocker? Most of our customers replace 3–4 separate tools, so it actually saves money overall."

- **"We don't have budget right now."**
  → "Understood. Would it help to start with the Starter plan at $49/mo? It covers the core features and you can upgrade anytime. We also have annual plans that save 20%."

- **"Your competitor is cheaper."**
  → "Good point — they usually are for one specific feature. But when you add up what you'd need from them to match what we offer (analytics + lead scoring + LLM ranking + heatmaps), it ends up costing more. Want me to break down the comparison?"

## Interest but Hesitation
- **"I need to think about it."**
  → "Of course — no rush. Would it help to see a quick demo so you have all the info? I can set one up in 15 minutes at a time that works for you."

- **"I need to talk to my team."**
  → "Makes sense. Want me to put together a one-pager you can share with them? And I'm happy to jump on a call with your team if that'd help."

- **"Send me more info."**
  → "Happy to! What's the best email? And what's the most important thing to include — pricing, features, or case studies?"

## Already Using Something Else
- **"We already have tools for this."**
  → "Most of our customers were too — usually 3–4 different tools. The pain they kept hitting was context-switching and data living in silos. Out of curiosity, what's the one thing your current setup doesn't do that you wish it did?"

## Not Interested
- **"Not interested."**
  → "Got it, appreciate you letting me know. If anything changes, we're here. Have a great day!"

## Rules
1. Never be pushy or aggressive — if they say no twice, respect it.
2. Always acknowledge the objection before responding.
3. Use questions to understand the real concern behind the stated objection.
4. If a competitor is mentioned, avoid badmouthing — focus on our unique value.
